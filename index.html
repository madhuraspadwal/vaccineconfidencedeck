The Problem

The information deficit model has failed

Public health messaging assumes vaccine hesitancy is a knowledge problem ‚Äî "if they just understood the science, they'd vaccinate."

But research shows hesitancy is primarily identity-protective cognition ‚Äî parents protecting their sense of self as thoughtful, careful caregivers.

Current interventions trigger defensiveness
  • Fact-checking feels like being told you're wrong
  • "Debunking" implies you've been fooled
  • Expert authority feels like pressure, not partnership

Our Solution

A calm space where parents can think through concerns without pressure

What it is
  • Uses motivational interviewing and deep canvassing ‚Äî proven to shift attitudes durably
  • Addresses the mental models underneath concerns, not just surface questions
  • Interactive explorations where users discover insights rather than being told
What it isn't
  • Not a quiz or assessment
  • Not a fact dump
  • Not a persuasive funnel
What changes if this works
  • Parents feel heard, not lectured
  • They leave with clearer thinking ‚Äî even if they don't change their mind
  • They have better questions to bring to their pediatrician
  • Trust is built, not eroded
The shift: From "convince them they're wrong" ‚Üí "help them think more clearly about what they already believe"

Prototype Evolution

Prototype 1: The Evidence Garden

A visual "garden" where each vaccine study was a bubble. Users could explore the evidence landscape ‚Äî hover over studies, see sample sizes, understand the weight of evidence.

The assumption: if parents could understand evidence literacy, they'd make informed decisions.

What we discovered
  • Evidence literacy wasn't the problem. Parents were protecting their identity as careful caregivers.
  • More evidence triggered more defensiveness, not less
  • The approach assumed an information deficit that didn't exist

The pivot: From evidence literacy ‚Üí identity-protective cognition. Drew on frameworks from pediatrics, social psychology, and health communication.

Prototype Evolution

Prototype 2: Structured journey with LLM

If it's about identity, not information, we need emotionally grounded conversations. Built a structured journey with LLM handling conversation.

What we learned
  • LLM-generated visuals were thin or nonsensical
  • Scenes repeated same beats with vague descriptions
  • Need to split the labor: humans design, LLM converses

The pivot: Hand-design Explorable Explanations per mental model. LLM only fills small slots (setup, affirmations). Router maps concerns ‚Üí mental models ‚Üí EE sequence.

User Testing

What we learned from real users

Surprising patterns
  • Users spent longer in wrap-up (avg 6 min vs. expected 2-3)
  • Most common question: trust/pharma ‚Äî even when stated concern differed
  • "Lighter than before" selected by 4/5 users
What we changed
  • Added more substantive factual responses
  • Made "ready to wrap up" button appear later
  • Simplified closing (removed confusing animation)

DebunkBot's approach

Evidence-based text dialogue that "debunks" conspiracy theories. Effective for misinformation (20% belief reduction). But vaccine hesitancy is identity-protective cognition ‚Äî facts alone can backfire.

Our approach

Start with emotion. Validate identity. Use interactive visuals so users discover insights themselves. Never frame as "debunking." Success = clearer reasoning.
